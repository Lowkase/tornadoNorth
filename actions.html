TORNADO NORTH

TAKE ACTION

Bad weather is on the way, knowing what to do will improve your safety.

HEED WARNINGS
Do
Listen to local forecasts and warnings. Watch live radar if severe thunderstorms are in your vicinity.
Don't
Don't ignore watches and warnings. If a warning is issued that means a tornado is occuring, take shelter immediately.
WATCH THE SKY
Do
Keep an eye out for the warning signs of an impending tornado including; wall clouds, very dark or green skies, intense winds, hail, debris and the "loud roar".
Don't
Ignore the changing conditions outside. Be vigilant, if you have to take shelter immediatly where will you go?
SEEK SHELTER
Do
Seek shelter in a basement or the lowest interior room or space possible. The underside of stairwells offers good protection.
Don't
Go near windows or outside walls. Do not be tempted to take pictures or videos of severe weather outside, if debris starts flying or falling you could be severely injured.
Do
Leave mobile homes as quickly as possible and find a sturdier shelter or lie flat in a low lying area away from large objects as best possible. Cover your head with your arms.
Don't
Stay in a mobile home, most tornadoes can destroy mobile homes. Flying debris can easily puncture the walls of a mobile home.
Do
Attempt to drive away from a tornado at a right angle if traffic is light. Otherwise, leave your vehicle and find shelter or lie flat in a low lying area away from large objects as best possible. Cover your head with your arms.
Don't
Seek shelter under a bridge or near trees or cars. The underside of bridges can intensify winds and large objects like trees or cars can fall on you.
Do
Use stairs when moving to the lowest level of your shelter.
Don't
Use an elevator.
