TORNADO NORTH

RECOGNIZE

Your first line of defence is recognizing the patterns that are associated with tornadoes.

1
WEATHER FORECASTS

Follow a daily weather forecast on the internet, tv or radio. When tornado like weather is predicted be vigilant and pay attention to the sky.

Image: The Weather Network Canada
  • The Weather Network
  • Weather Underground
  • CBC Weather
2
ADVISORIES / WATCHES / WARNINGS

Pay attention to news sources and your cell phone for weather updates.

ADVISORY - May be issued for a wide area and indicates deteriorating weather conditions.

WATCH - Are issued when conditions that severe weather is forming favoring tornado formation.

WARNING - A tornado is known to be occurring or is about to occur. Find shelter immediately and take cover in a basement or an inside part of a structure that is away from windows. Not near a sturdy shelter? take cover in a low lying area like a ditch to avoid flying debris.

3
REAL TIME DATA

Weather radar can give a clear indication of where thunderstorm cells are located in relation to you. Knowing the direction tornado like weather is headed can help you take action to protect yourself.

Image: The Weather Network Canada
  • Wundermap
  • The Weather Network
  • Weather Bug
4
WALL CLOUD

If a wall cloud or a lowering of the base of a thunderstorm is observed it is a very good indication of a pending tornado, even more so if the wall cloud is slowly rotating.

Image: Mr. Twister
Youtube Video: Jonh Farley
5
VERY DARK AND/OR GREEN SKY

Clouds associated with tornadic system will appear very dark. Sunlight refracts off of hail giving the sky a green tint.

Image: James Smart
6
INTENSE WINDS / QUIET

Intense winds that may quickly shift are associated with tornadoes.

Sometimes just before a tornado strikes the wind may die dow and the aire will become very still, you will not hear wildlife sounds like birds singing.

Youtube Video: Brandon Ivey
7
HAIL

A storm's updraft may create hail and is located immediately above a tornado. Large hail is a sign that a tornado is imminent.

Youtube Video: Thumbnail
8
DEBRIS

An approaching cloud of debris may precede a tornados imminent strike, even if a funnel is not visible.

Video: The Weather Network
8
LOUD ROAR

A tornado creates a loud roar, many observers liken it to the sound of a freight train.
