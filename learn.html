TORNADO NORTH

Canada has the most Tornadoes in the world, second only to the United States

On average there are approximately 80 confirmed and unconfirmed tornados in Canada every year. The majority of these tornadoes touch down in southern portions of the prairies (Alberta, Saskatchewan and Manitoba), Ontario and Quebec.

Every province in Canada is susceptible to tornadoes, however the occurrences of tornadoes diminishes as one travels north.

Tornadoes form in large thunderstorm called a supercell. Warm and cold air interact inside a supercell to produce a large swirling mass called a mesocyclone. As the warm and cold air interactions continue the the mesocyclone may intensify into a tornado that can reach the ground. This infographic the steps of how a tornado forms.
